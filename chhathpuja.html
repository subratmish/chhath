Chhath Puja

The ancient festival dedicated to the Sun God and Chhathi Maiya

The Sacred Festival

Chhath Puja is one of the oldest Hindu festivals, with references found in the Rigveda. The festival is dedicated to the Sun God (Surya) and his consort Usha (Dawn) and Pratyusha (Dusk).

Archaeological evidence suggests that the Sun worship traditions date back to the Indus Valley Civilization, making Chhath one of the most ancient forms of worship still practiced today.

Mythology & History

Ancient stories that shaped the festival

Mahabharata Connection

According to Hindu mythology, Chhath Puja was first performed by Draupadi and the Pandavas to solve their problems and regain their lost kingdom. The Sun God granted their wishes after they performed the rigorous puja with devotion.

Another legend mentions Karna, the warrior from Mahabharata, who was blessed by Surya and gained his legendary powers through Sun worship.

Regional Origins

Originally practiced in the ancient kingdoms of Magadh (modern-day Bihar), Chhath Puja has evolved over millennia while maintaining its core essence. The festival represents humanity's gratitude toward nature and the life-giving force of the Sun.

The tradition was carried forward by generations of devotees, particularly from the Bihar and Eastern Uttar Pradesh regions, and has now spread globally wherever these communities have settled.

The Four Sacred Days

Each day of Chhath Puja has specific rituals and significance

1
DAY

Nahay Khay

Purification Day

This day symbolizes purification of body, mind, and soul before approaching the Sun God. Devotees take holy baths and consume sattvic (pure) food prepared without salt, onion, or garlic.

2
DAY

Lohanda/Kharna

Fasting Day

This day tests the devotee's determination and strengthens their spiritual resolve. Devotees observe nirjala vrat (waterless fast) throughout the day and break it in the evening after offering kheer to the moon.

3
DAY

Sandhya Arghya

Evening Offering

The most visually spectacular day, symbolizing gratitude for the day's blessings. Thousands of devotees gather at water bodies to offer prayers to the setting sun, creating an unforgettable spiritual atmosphere.

4
DAY

Usha Arghya

Dawn Offering

The culmination of the festival, welcoming new beginnings and divine blessings. Devotees offer prayers to the rising sun and break their 36-hour fast with the blessed prasad.

Unique Significance

What makes Chhath Puja special

Direct Sun Worship

Chhath Puja is unique as it worships the Sun God directly, recognizing the sun as the source of all life and energy.

Gender Inclusivity

The festival demonstrates remarkable gender inclusivity, with both men and women performing the same rigorous rituals.

Environmental Harmony

The festival promotes environmental consciousness through natural offerings and worship near water bodies.

Traditional Offerings

Thekua

The most important prasad made with wheat flour, jaggery, and ghee. Prepared with utmost devotion and offered to the Sun God.

Fruits & Sweets

Fresh seasonal fruits, especially sugarcane, bananas, and coconuts, along with traditional sweets like kheer and laddu.

Natural Items

Bamboo baskets, earthen lamps, and other eco-friendly items that reflect the festival's connection with nature.

Modern Relevance

How Chhath Puja remains relevant today

Community Building

Chhath Puja strengthens family bonds as entire families participate together, with different generations contributing to preparations and rituals, fostering cultural continuity.

Mental Discipline

The rigorous fasting and ritual observances develop spiritual discipline, mental strength, and the ability to endure hardships for higher purposes.

Global Unity

The festival brings communities together, transcending social and economic barriers. Collective celebrations foster unity and mutual support among devotees worldwide.

Sustainability

The emphasis on natural, biodegradable offerings and water body worship promotes environmental awareness and sustainable living practices.

Where Chhath is Celebrated

From Bihar to the world

Bihar & Jharkhand

The heartland of Chhath Puja where traditions are most authentic and elaborate.

Mumbai & Delhi

Large migrant communities maintain traditions with adaptations to urban environments.

Global Diaspora

Celebrated by Indian diaspora worldwide in countries like USA, UK, Australia, and Nepal.

Modern Times

Contemporary practices balancing tradition with modern realities and urban constraints.
