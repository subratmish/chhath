About Chhath Utsav Mahasangh

Our Mission

Founded in 1993, Chhath Utsav Mahasangh is a cultural and social organization dedicated to preserving the sacred traditions of Chhath Puja. We strive to provide a safe, inclusive, and environmentally conscious environment for thousands of devotees each year.

Our mission extends beyond organizing festivals - we work year-round to promote cultural awareness, environmental sustainability, and community welfare among the Bihari diaspora in Mumbai.

Our Vision

To create a harmonious celebration that honors ancient traditions while embracing modern values of environmental protection and social inclusion.

  • Preserve authentic Chhath Puja rituals
  • Promote environmental consciousness
  • Foster community unity and cultural pride
  • Ensure safety and accessibility for all devotees

Our Journey Through Time

1993 - Foundation

Chhath Utsav Mahasangh was founded by a small group of dedicated volunteers from the Bihari community in Mumbai. The first celebration was held at Juhu Beach with just 200 devotees.

1997 - Official Registration

The organization was officially registered as a non-profit entity. Received first civic approvals for beach celebrations and established safety protocols.

2001 - First Thousand

Crossed the milestone of 1,000 devotees participating in the four-day celebration. Introduced organized prasad distribution and volunteer coordination system.

2005 - Infrastructure Development

Built the first permanent ghat facilities at Juhu Beach. Installed lighting systems and established medical aid stations for devotee safety.

2010 - Environmental Initiative

Launched "Clean Chhath" campaign promoting eco-friendly celebrations. Introduced biodegradable offerings and beach cleanup drives.

2015 - Digital Transformation

Launched official website and social media presence. Introduced online volunteer registration and event coordination systems.

2020 - Pandemic Adaptation

Successfully conducted COVID-compliant celebrations with safety protocols. Introduced live streaming for devotees unable to attend physically.

2023 - 30th Anniversary

Celebrated three decades of service with over 50,000 devotees participating. Received recognition from Mumbai Municipal Corporation for cultural preservation.
